1. Overview

  • Duration: ~60 minutes

2. Warm-up & Revision

Short revision on for / since / ago to reactivate previous knowledge.

  • Complete the sentences using for / since / ago
  • Quick correction + explanation
  • Focus on natural time expressions (e.g. an hour ago instead of one hour ago)

3. Grammar Focus – Present Perfect vs Past Simple

We reviewed the difference between the two tenses:

  • Past Simple: finished actions + specific time (yesterday, in 2020, last week…)
  • Present Perfect: experiences, duration until now, changes, recent news

Linked grammar sheet:

  • Present Perfect vs Past Simple – Grammar Sheet

4. Exercises done during the lesson

a) Targeted exercises

  • Choose the correct tense (Present Perfect / Past Simple)
  • Use of ever / never / already / yet
  • Correction of typical tense-mixing mistakes

b) Mixed revision

  • Tenses + for / since / ago
  • Combining several grammar points in one sentence
  • Attention to question formation and negative forms

c) Writing task

  • Topic: Your arrival in Australia and your first steps
  • Use Past Simple (events) + Present Perfect (results / experiences)
  • Detailed sentence-by-sentence correction
  • “Native-like” rewritten version provided

5. Feedback

Strengths

  • Clear understanding of when to use Past Simple vs Present Perfect
  • Good use of for / since / ago in context
  • Fast and accurate self-correction after feedback
  • Improved clarity and structure in writing

Points to improve

  • Collocations: use start looking for a job (not begin seeking for)
  • No space before ? and ! in English
  • Present Perfect with since for situations that continue
  • Uncountable nouns: homework, information, furniture, advice

6. Useful vocabulary & expressions from the lesson

  • to start looking for a job – commencer à chercher du travail
  • to do the paperwork – faire la paperasse / démarches
  • I’ve just finished…
  • I’ve lived here for / since…
  • Have you ever been to…?
